# Encrypt Password

## Securely Encrypt and Store Your Passwords

Managing dozens of passwords across different websites, apps, and services can be difficult. Instead of storing your passwords in plain text, **Encrypt Password** lets you encrypt them using your own secret passkey, so they remain unreadable to anyone without that passkey.

Whether you have personal accounts, work logins, banking credentials, or social media passwords, you can encrypt them before saving them anywhere you choose. Once encrypted, the output can be stored in notes, cloud storage, email drafts, USB drives, password files, or any other location. Even if someone gains access to the encrypted data, they cannot read your original password without the correct passkey.

## How It Works

1. Enter the password you want to protect.
2. Create or enter your secret passkey.
3. Encrypt the password.
4. Save the encrypted text wherever you prefer.
5. Whenever you need your original password again, return to the website, enter the encrypted text along with the same passkey, and decrypt it.

The website does not require you to remember dozens of passwords. Instead, you only need to keep your encrypted passwords and securely remember your secret passkey.

## Why Use Encryption?

* Protect sensitive passwords from being stored as plain text.
* Store encrypted passwords almost anywhere without exposing the original values.
* Keep your login credentials private, even if your storage location is compromised.
* Easily decrypt your passwords whenever you need them using your secret passkey.
* Simple, fast, and easy to use for anyone.

## Important Security Notice

Your **passkey is the most important part of the encryption process**.

* **Never lose your passkey.**
* **Never share your passkey with anyone you do not trust.**
* Without the correct passkey, your encrypted password cannot be decrypted.
* If you forget or lose your passkey, there is no way to recover your original password from the encrypted data.

For the best security, choose a strong, unique passkey that is difficult for others to guess. Consider storing your passkey in a secure location that only you can access.

## Best Practices

* Use a different passkey for highly sensitive information if needed.
* Keep backups of your encrypted passwords.
* Do not store your passkey together with your encrypted passwords.
* Regularly update important account passwords and re-encrypt them if they change.
* Always verify that you are using the correct passkey before deleting your original password.

## Disclaimer

Keep your passphrase safe. If you lose it, your encrypted data cannot be recovered.
